## Summary
This 5-day trip itinerary is designed for a moderate-paced journey to Tokyo and Kyoto, Japan. The plan focuses on exploring temples, enjoying local food, and avoiding crowded areas. The trip includes a balance of cultural experiences, historic landmarks, and natural scenery. Plan based on model knowledge; not verified against live sources.

## Day-by-day
- Day 1: Arrive in Tokyo, visit Meiji Shrine in the early morning, and explore the Shimokitazawa neighborhood for affordable food options.
- Day 2: Visit Tsukiji Outer Market for fresh sushi and seafood, then head to the Imperial Palace for a stroll around the beautiful grounds.
- Day 3: Travel to Kyoto by Bullet Train (approximately 2.5 hours), and check-in to a hotel in the Gion neighborhood. Explore the traditional temples and authentic food options in the area.
- Day 4: Hike the famous orange torii gate trail at Fushimi Inari Shrine in the early morning, then visit Kinkaku-ji Temple to admire the stunning golden pavilion.
- Day 5: Take a peaceful walk through the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, and explore Nijo Castle to learn about its history and 'nightingale floors'.

## Where to stay
In Tokyo, consider staying in the Shimokitazawa neighborhood for its affordable food options and proximity to temples. In Kyoto, the Gion neighborhood is recommended for its traditional temples, authentic food options, and scenic atmosphere.

## Logistics
The transfer from Tokyo to Kyoto will be by Bullet Train on Day 3, with a travel time of approximately 2.5 hours. Booking in advance is recommended to secure seats.
